Introduction

When planning a remote business meeting, the convener must engage in the 'Rules of Engagement' as a form of etiquette, recognised across business societies has, the 'manner' in which people behave to others whilst communicating.

The convener will appoint a chairperson to lead the meeting, and a secretary to organise the agenda, participants, time line, materials, and software applications, required for the meeting to be sucessful, the secretary will also take the meeting notes [minutes] and record the attendees, their contributions and establish the dateline for the follow-up meeting.

Rules of Engagement

Netiquette is the internets version of ettiquette,as a guideline for how individuals should act when communicating with others.Participants are required to show Politeness, consideration, Mute their microphone when not talking, understand the agenda,be prepared,signal to others you are in the meeting,don't talk over other people,concentrate on the meeting.

Constraints of collabrative Technology

Agenda An agenda would include; timeline,attendees,topics,history of topics,support documents, and an allotted time for each topic.

Timeline The secretary would be responsible for inviting the attendees contacting individuals to agree the meetings date is available, the time and the location [including the necessary software and connections].

Attendees there would be a list of people who are expected to attend the meeting, their topic/theme/trade/profession, the name of their company.

Topics topics could include, different trades, professional services, datelines,quality,resources.

History would show the progress of each topic, assessed at prior meeting.

Support documents a collection of notes,documents,samples,literature of topics, to support the understanding of ongoing topic problems.

Allotted time emphasising how much time each topic the group, can discuss the issue's, in order to complete the agenda within the allotted time of the meeting.
